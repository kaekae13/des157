Audience Persona

Emily Zhou

Demographics

  • Age: 22
  • Gender: F
  • Location: NY to SF/Hawaii (Maui)
  • Education: B.A. in Psychology (USF)
  • Family: middle-class/middle income

Job

Emily works as a counselor at the Kids' Club at Fairmont Hotels. She supervises kids at day camps and leads various activities including circle time, story time, volcano making, arts and crafts, etc.

Goals and Challenges

Her goals are primarily to distract herself from any kind of distress as well as anxiety or even depression, as well as to find others experiencing similar issues. For example, Tumblr is a frequent site for her because it is a popular platform for others who were also experiencing depression or anxiety, and made her feel more understood and less alone in her emotional struggles. She also goes to music apps (Spotify, Soundcloud), as well as watching/listening to ASMR videos on Youtube for the same purposes.



Her challenges with achieving her goals with the existing applications and web interfaces she uses is that they oftentimes do little to actually relieve her stress/anxiety because she chooses to listen to certain music or look at certain posts that instead reemphasize and worsen her emotional state



In Emily's case, an application that focused solely on changing or reducing these emotions would be much more successful. Though the majority of my background research focuses on students with learning disabilities rather than just mental health issues, I feel that students with learning disabilities would also benefit from such an app. An immense pressure to keep up with the curriculum in addition to their significantly different learning processes can create feelings of stress and anxiety in the classroom environment - a phenomenon that is quite common with dyslexic and dysgraphic students, as well as those with AD/HD.

Values and Fears

Emily values self expression, which is why she frequents social media apps where individuals can express who they are and interact with others. Going along that line, Emily also enjoys other applications where people can express their musical creativity (Soundcloud) as well as online content communities such as Youtube where users can view, share, and create their own content.In terms of fears, Emily does not enjoy apps that are too fixed or limited in their content.
